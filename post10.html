Overview

As part of the application process for my UI/UX Design Internship at TruckerSay, I was given a design challenge to create a social media web app for trucking professionals. The brief was intentionally open-ended, with only two main constraints: the design must be limited to 10 Figma screens or less, and the scope and functionality of the platform were up to me to define.

This challenge tested my ability to scope a project, apply design thinking, and translate user needs into solutions. Since the trucking industry has unique communication and community needs, I focused on designing an experience that would help drivers connect, share updates, and exchange knowledge in a simple, approachable way.

The project served as both a demonstration of my design process and my ability to communicate my decisions clearly during an interview.

Role: UI/UX Designer

Brand: TruckerSay

Project Type: Mobile Application Design with Figma

UX Research

Since I didn't have time for deep fieldwork, I conducted lightweight research focused on understanding trucking professionals’ needs and opportunities for a community platform. My research included:

  • Review of TruckerSay’s Website: Identified existing features like news, mentorship, and community, then explored how they could be reimagined for a social media experience.
  • Competitive Analysis: Looked at LinkedIn for networking patterns, Instagram for social interactions (feed, likes, sharing), and trucking forums to understand community discussions.
  • Heuristic Review: Read trucking forums from Reddit to identify common needs that would support usability for trucking professionals.
  • User Needs Hypotheses: Long-haul truckers need quick connection tools, new drivers need mentorship, and industry professionals want reliable news and updates.
  • Design Constraints: With a 10-screen limit, I focused on my main features to create a feasable social platform centered on community, news, and mentorship.

UI/UX Design

UI Kit

After establishing the scope and main features, I created a UI kit for consistency across screens. Since this was a design challenge, I wanted to balance speed with reusable elements. I drew inspiration from the TruckerSay website to keep my design aligned with their existing brand identity:

  • Color Palette: Adapted TruckerSay’s primary colors to create a clean interface while ensuring contrast for accessibility.
  • Typography: Referenced the site’s font choices and hierarchy, simplifying them for readability for mobile screens.
  • Buttons & Icons: Drew from the site’s iconography style to maintain visual consistency.
  • Components: Built modular elements like cards, navigation bars, and buttons for consistency.

Top ↑

Final App Designs

After creating low-fi wireframes in Figma to map out the user flows, I layered in elements from my UI kit to develop high-fi screens. This approach allowed me to establish my structure first, then focus on visual consistency and interactivity.

With the typography, color, and components in place, I connected the final designs into an interactive prototype for submission.

Final Thoughts

This design challenge gave me the opportunity to practice the full design process under real constraints. Working within the 10-screen limit taught me how to prioritize essential features and design for clarity, while still keeping the experience engaging for users. The project not only helped me secure the UI/UX internship at TruckerSay, but also reinforced the importance of process-driven design and balancing user needs, brand identity, and usability in every design decision.
