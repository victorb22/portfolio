AggiePass Mobile App

Date: September 2023

Background

Context: Davis has a multitude of food options, from the Coho, to the Silo, to dining halls, and food trucks. My group and I wanted to create an exciting digital platform that rewards current students for visiting their favorite on-campus dining spots.

Role: UI/UX Designer

Brand: Design Interactive

Project Type: Mobile Application Design with Figma

UX Research

Methods:

  • Competitor Analysis
  • Usability Testing
  • Prototype Testing
  • UCD Student Interviews

Pain Points: Students expressed that there was not an accessible and convenient platform to access information for dining at UC Davis. We also discovered that there was a lack of motivation to encourage on-campus food purchases, and this helped us recognize the importance for a digital ordering platform integrated with a reward system.

Methods:

  • Competitor Analysis
  • Usability Testing
  • Prototype Testing
  • UCD Student Interviews

UX Personas

We began by creating a User Persona based off data received from 5 different UCD students to get a better understanding of our potential users.

User Flow

Using this information and other research, we began to ideate for a User Flow that would match what UCD students are looking for in a campus dining app.

UI Kit

We began creating a UI Kit for our app. We used a different color palette than what is commonly associated with UCD in order to make it stand out, however, we still utilized the UCD's font to maintain some identity to the school.

Low-Fidelity Design

We then began creating our Mid Fidelity Design for our app and testing which features would be best to include.

Final Design

Final Thoughts: This was what the final design of the log in and sign up to the app looked like. My takeaways from this project were that I learned to identify preferences among UCD students regarding on-campus dining, as well as to recognize pain points in existing digital platforms
